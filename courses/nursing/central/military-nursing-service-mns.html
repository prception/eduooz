What is Military Nursing Service?

The Military Nursing Service (MNS) is a direct entry path for registered nurses to join the Indian Armed Forces, serving in military hospitals, field units, and specialized healthcare wings of the Army, Navy, and Air Force as Commissioned Officers.

Central Government Recruitment Conducted by DGAFMS
Commissioned Officer Post Direct entry into Military Nursing Service
Serve Across All Three Forces Army, Navy & Air Force hospitals

Career Growth Promotions, training & international postings

01

Online Application Submission

Candidates must apply online through the official Military Nursing Service (MNS) / DGAFMS portal during the notified application period, filling personal, academic, and professional details accurately.

02

Application Fee Payment

Pay the application fee online according to the post or category (UR/OBC/EWS/SC/ST/Ex-Servicemen). Payment is required to complete the application process and confirm registration.

03

Application Status & Document Upload

After successful registration, candidates can check application status and upload scanned photographs, signatures, and other required documents as per Military Nurse / MNS guidelines.

04

Entrance Examination (CBT / Written Test)

Eligible candidates appear for the Computer-Based Test (CBT) or written examination on the scheduled date. The examination assesses nursing knowledge relevant to military healthcare, including medical-surgical nursing, maternal-child health, community health, mental health, and specialty nursing areas.

05

Shortlisting for Merit List / Score Publication

Based on examination performance, candidates are shortlisted for interview or appointment. Scores or merit lists are published on the official Military Nursing Service (MNS) / DGAFMS website.

06

Document Verification & Medical Examination

Shortlisted candidates must produce original documents for verification and may undergo a detailed medical fitness examination as required by the post and Armed Forces standards.

07

Final Merit List & Appointment

The final merit list is prepared based on examination scores, interview (if applicable), document verification, and medical fitness. Selected candidates are appointed as Military Nurses in Indian Armed Forces hospitals and healthcare establishments according to merit, reservation, and available vacancies.

Everything you need to know.

From exam eligibility to study strategies, find answers to your most pressing MNS preparation questions.

A Military Nurse is a qualified nursing professional who serves in the Armed Forces Medical Services (AFMS), Military Hospitals, Command Hospitals, and defense healthcare facilities. Military Nurses provide medical care to armed forces personnel, veterans, and their families.

Candidates with B.Sc Nursing, Post Basic B.Sc Nursing, or M.Sc Nursing qualifications from recognized institutions and valid Nursing Council registration are generally eligible to apply for Military Nurse Recruitment, subject to official eligibility criteria.

The required qualification for Military Nurse vacancies typically includes B.Sc Nursing, Post Basic B.Sc Nursing, or M.Sc Nursing from a recognized university or institution approved by the Indian Nursing Council.

The Military Nurse selection process generally includes a written examination, Computer-Based Test (CBT), interview, medical examination, document verification, and merit-based selection.

The Military Nursing Service Exam Pattern generally includes objective-type questions covering nursing subjects, general English, biology, chemistry, physics, reasoning ability, and aptitude depending on the recruitment notification.

Yes, valid registration with the State Nursing Council or Indian Nursing Council is generally mandatory for Military Nursing Service and Military Nurse eligibility.

Applicants generally need educational certificates, Nursing Council registration certificate, identity proof, age proof, domicile certificate, passport-size photographs, signature, and other supporting documents.

Military Nurses provide patient care, emergency medical support, critical care services, surgical nursing assistance, rehabilitation care, health education, and healthcare management in military hospitals and defense healthcare facilities.

Military Nurse jobs offer government job security, attractive salary packages, free medical facilities, accommodation benefits, pension benefits, travel allowances, career growth opportunities, and national service pride.

Military Nurse jobs offer a rewarding healthcare career, excellent salary packages, government benefits, leadership opportunities, professional growth, and the chance to serve the nation through military healthcare services.
