What is ESIC Nursing Officer?

The ESIC Nursing Officer Exam is a central government recruitment examination conducted by the Employees' State Insurance Corporation (ESIC) for recruitment of Nursing Officers in ESIC hospitals and dispensaries across India. It is a centralized, merit-based examination designed to hire qualified and skilled nursing professionals in the public health sector — ensuring efficient healthcare delivery under the Employees' State Insurance Scheme for insured workers and their families across the country.

Who Can Apply & Age Criteria

Eligibility & Age Requirements.

Eligibility Requirements

Degrees and Recognition Required

Must hold B.Sc. Nursing (Basic) or Post Basic B.Sc. Nursing from a recognized university or institution. The degree must be recognized by the Indian Nursing Council (INC). Must be registered as a Registered Nurse & Registered Midwife (RNRM) with the State Nursing Council.

Valid and Active Professional Registration

Registration must be maintained with the State Nursing Council. Registration must be valid at the time of application and appointment. Candidates must ensure their RNRM certificate is current and not expired at the time of applying for ESIC recruitment.

Clinical Experience (Preferred or Required)

Some ESIC notifications may prefer 1 year of clinical experience after B.Sc. Nursing. Experience should be in a hospital, community health centre, or recognized nursing institution. Refer to the specific ESIC recruitment notification for the applicable experience requirement.

Age Limit & Relaxation

Typically 21–30 years for General category candidates (refer to the latest ESIC notification for exact limits). Age relaxation is applicable for SC/ST/OBC/PwBD and other eligible categories as per Government of India rules. Age is calculated on the date specified in the ESIC notification.

Nationality Requirements

Applicant must be an Indian citizen. Must fulfill all eligibility requirements as specified in the ESIC notification for the relevant post and location.

Medical Fitness Requirements

Candidate must be medically fit for the post. A medical fitness certificate may be required during document verification or appointment procedures as per ESIC standards.

Age Limit by Category

General Category

21–30 years

Must be within the upper age limit specified in the ESIC notification. Upper age is typically 30 years as on the last date of online application submission as per the recruitment advertisement.

SC / ST

35 years

+5 years relaxation over General category as per Government of India rules. Applicable to candidates belonging to Scheduled Caste / Scheduled Tribe communities.

OBC (Non-Creamy Layer)

33 years

+3 years relaxation over General category as per Government of India rules. Must possess valid Non-Creamy Layer certificate issued by competent authority as per ESIC notification.

Persons with Benchmark Disabilities (PwBD)

40 years

Age relaxation as per Government of India / ESIC notification, typically up to 10 years. Must have minimum 40% disability certified by designated medical board.

Ex-Servicemen / Defence Candidates

Varies

Age relaxation equal to the period of military service rendered, as per ESIC notification and Government of India rules. Applicable to discharged defence personnel as per extant orders.

Widows / Divorcees

Varies

Eligible for age relaxation as per ESIC notification and reservation guidelines. Applicable to widows and judicially divorced women who have not remarried, as specified in the recruitment advertisement.

Other Eligible Reserved Categories

Varies

Age relaxation and benefits as prescribed by Government of India rules or the ESIC notification. Refer to the official ESIC advertisement for specific category-wise relaxations applicable to the post.

01

Online Application Submission

Candidates must apply online through the official ESIC portal during the notified application period, filling in personal, academic, and professional details accurately as per the ESIC recruitment advertisement.

02

Application Fee Payment

Pay the application fee online according to the category (UR/OBC/EWS/SC/ST/Ex-Servicemen). Payment is required to complete the application process as per ESIC guidelines and the specific recruitment notification.

03

Application Status & Document Upload

Check application status after registration. Upload scanned photograph, signature, and other required documents as per ESIC guidelines. Ensure all uploads meet the specified format and size requirements.

04

Computer-Based Test (CBT)

Eligible candidates appear for the CBT on the scheduled date. The CBT assesses Nursing knowledge, General Awareness, Arithmetic, General Intelligence, and Reasoning as per the ESIC Nursing Officer recruitment pattern.

05

Shortlisting & Score Publication

Based on CBT marks, candidates are shortlisted for Document Verification (DV). Scores are published on the official ESIC website for candidates to verify their selection status and cutoff standing.

06

Document Verification & Medical Examination

Shortlisted candidates must produce original documents for verification and undergo a medical examination as per ESIC standards. Educational certificates, nursing registration, and category documents are thoroughly verified.

07

Final Merit List & Appointment

The final merit list is prepared based on CBT marks, document verification, and medical fitness. Selected candidates are appointed as Nursing Officers in ESIC hospitals and dispensaries across India as per merit, reservation norms, and available vacancies.

The ESIC Nursing Officer Exam is a competitive recruitment examination conducted for the selection of Nursing Officers in hospitals and healthcare institutions under the Employees' State Insurance Corporation (ESIC). The exam assesses candidates' nursing knowledge, professional skills, and aptitude.

Candidates with B.Sc Nursing, Post Basic B.Sc Nursing, or GNM qualifications along with valid Nursing Council registration and prescribed experience requirements, if applicable, are generally eligible to apply for ESIC Nursing Officer recruitment.

The educational qualification for ESIC Nursing Officer vacancies typically includes B.Sc Nursing, Post Basic B.Sc Nursing, or Diploma in General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM) from a recognized institution.

The ESIC Nursing Officer selection process may include a written examination, Computer-Based Test (CBT), merit list preparation, document verification, and medical examination depending on the recruitment notification.

The ESIC Nursing Officer exam pattern usually consists of objective-type multiple-choice questions covering nursing subjects, professional knowledge, reasoning ability, general awareness, and aptitude.
