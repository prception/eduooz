01

Online Application

Apply through the official Railway Recruitment Board (RRB) portal or the RRB zonal website during the notified application window. Select your preferred zone and fill in personal, academic, and registration details accurately.

02

Application Fee Payment

Pay the application fee online as per category (UR/OBC/EWS/SC/ST/Ex-SM). Fee concessions apply for Women, SC/ST, PwBD, and ex-servicemen candidates. Payment can be made via net banking, debit card, or UPI.

03

Admit Card Download

Download your RRB Staff Nurse admit card from the official RRB zonal portal prior to the examination date. Carry both the printed admit card and a valid original government photo ID to the examination hall.

04

Computer-Based Test (CBT)

Appear for the RRB Staff Nurse CBT at the allotted exam center. The exam covers Professional Knowledge (Nursing), General Awareness, General Intelligence & Reasoning, and General Science. Questions are typically multiple-choice with no negative marking.

05

Result and Merit List

RRB publishes CBT results on the official portal. Merit list is drawn category-wise based on normalized marks. Shortlisted candidates proceed to document verification.

06

Document Verification

Appear at the designated RRB office with original documents: educational certificates, nursing council registration, community/category certificate, date of birth proof, and identity documents. Railway employees require service certificates.

07

Medical Fitness and Appointment

After document verification, selected candidates undergo a medical fitness examination as per Railway Medical Standards. Medically fit candidates receive appointment orders and join Indian Railways as permanent Staff Nurses.

From exam eligibility to study strategies, find answers to your most pressing RRB Staff Nurse preparation questions.

The RRB Staff Nurse Exam is a national-level recruitment examination conducted by Railway Recruitment Board (RRB) to recruit Staff Nurses, Nursing Officers, and nursing professionals for various hospitals and healthcare units under Indian Railways.

Candidates who possess B.Sc Nursing, Post Basic B.Sc Nursing, GNM Nursing, or equivalent nursing qualifications from a recognized institution along with valid Nursing Council registration are generally eligible to apply for RRB Staff Nurse recruitment.

The educational qualification for RRB Staff Nurse vacancies typically includes B.Sc Nursing, General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM), or other nursing qualifications recognized by the Indian Nursing Council.

The RRB Staff Nurse selection process generally includes a Computer-Based Test (CBT), document verification, and medical fitness examination before final appointment.

The RRB Staff Nurse exam pattern generally consists of objective-type multiple-choice questions covering Professional Nursing Subjects, General Awareness, General Intelligence, Reasoning, Arithmetic, and General Science.

Yes, valid registration with the State Nursing Council or Indian Nursing Council is generally mandatory for RRB Staff Nurse eligibility.

Yes, RRB Staff Nurse examinations generally include negative marking for incorrect answers. Candidates should check the latest notification for the exact marking scheme.

Applicants generally need educational certificates, Nursing Council registration certificate, identity proof, age proof, category certificate (if applicable), passport-size photographs, and signature copies.

RRB Staff Nurses provide patient care, administer medications, assist doctors, maintain medical records, monitor patient health conditions, and support healthcare services in railway hospitals and health units.

RRB Staff Nurse jobs offer government job security, attractive salary packages, pension benefits as applicable, healthcare facilities, paid leave, promotion opportunities, and work-life balance.

RRB Staff Nurse jobs offer stable government employment, competitive salary packages, healthcare benefits, career advancement opportunities, and the chance to work with one of India's largest public sector organizations.
