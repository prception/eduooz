What is RCC Nursing Exam?

The Regional Cancer Centre (RCC) Nursing Exam is a central government recruitment examination conducted by one of the designated Regional Cancer Centres in India for the appointment of nursing professionals who will serve in cancer care units, oncology wards, and specialized rehabilitation departments. RCCs are premier government cancer treatment, research, and training centers recognized under the Government of India's health system (often linked to the Ministry of Health & Family Welfare).

This exam is designed to select qualified nurses specializing in oncology and cancer care, which requires advanced skills in supportive treatment, pain management, palliative care, and specialized nursing procedures. It recruits Nursing Officers / Staff Nurses specifically for oncology and cancer treatment units.

01

Online Application

Apply through the official Regional Cancer Centre (RCC) Thiruvananthapuram recruitment portal or PSC Kerala portal during the notified application period. Fill in accurate personal, educational, and nursing council registration details.

02

Application Fee Payment

Submit the application fee as per category (UR/OBC/SC/ST) via online payment on the recruitment portal. Keep the fee payment confirmation for reference.

03

Admit Card / Hall Ticket

Download the admit card/hall ticket from the RCC or Kerala PSC portal before the examination. Carry a printed copy along with a government-issued photo identity document to the exam venue.

04

Written Examination

Appear for the RCC Staff Nurse written examination. The test assesses nursing knowledge across medical-surgical, oncology, OBG, community health, pediatric, and mental health nursing. Questions are typically objective-type multiple choice.

05

Result and Merit List

Results are published on the official RCC or PSC portal. A category-wise merit list is prepared. Qualified candidates are called for the next stage of the selection process.

06

Document Verification

Shortlisted candidates must submit original documents: degree/diploma certificates, nursing council registration certificate, category/community certificate, experience certificates (if applicable), and identity proof.

07

Final Appointment

After verification, final selection is made and appointment orders are issued. Selected candidates join the Regional Cancer Centre Thiruvananthapuram as Staff Nurses in oncology and allied departments.

Everything you need to know.

From exam eligibility to study strategies, find answers to your most pressing RCC Staff Nurse preparation questions.

The RCC Nursing Officer Exam is a recruitment examination conducted by the Regional Cancer Centre (RCC) for the selection of Nursing Officers, Staff Nurses, and other nursing professionals. The exam evaluates candidates' nursing knowledge, clinical skills, and aptitude for oncology healthcare services.

Candidates with B.Sc Nursing, Post Basic B.Sc Nursing, GNM Nursing, or equivalent nursing qualifications from a recognized institution along with valid Nursing Council registration are generally eligible to apply.

The required qualification usually includes B.Sc Nursing, Post Basic B.Sc Nursing, GNM Nursing, or equivalent nursing qualifications recognized by the Indian Nursing Council and relevant authorities.

The selection process may include a written examination, Computer-Based Test (CBT), skill test, interview, document verification, and medical fitness assessment depending on the recruitment notification.

The RCC Nursing Officer Exam typically consists of objective-type multiple-choice questions covering nursing subjects, oncology nursing concepts, general knowledge, reasoning ability, and aptitude.

Yes, valid registration with the State Nursing Council or Indian Nursing Council is generally mandatory for RCC Nursing Officer eligibility.

Applicants generally need educational certificates, Nursing Council registration certificate, identity proof, age proof, category certificate (if applicable), passport-size photographs, signatures, and other supporting documents.

RCC Nursing Officers provide patient care, assist oncologists, administer medications, monitor cancer patients, support chemotherapy and radiotherapy services, maintain medical records, and ensure quality nursing care.

RCC Nursing Officer jobs offer government-sector benefits, attractive salary packages, oncology specialization exposure, healthcare benefits, career advancement opportunities, and professional development.

RCC Nursing Officer jobs offer excellent career opportunities in oncology nursing, competitive salaries, government benefits, advanced clinical exposure, professional growth, and the opportunity to work in a leading cancer care institution.
