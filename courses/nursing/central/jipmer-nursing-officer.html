What is JIPMER Nursing Officer Exam?

The JIPMER Nursing Officer Exam is a central government recruitment examination conducted by the Jawaharlal Institute of Postgraduate Medical Education & Research (JIPMER), Puducherry — an Institute of National Importance under the Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India. This exam recruits qualified nursing professionals for JIPMER's hospitals, specialty departments, and associated healthcare facilities across critical care, emergency, oncology, surgical, and community health settings.

Weeks 3–10

Review Regularly and Reinforce Key Concepts

Weeks 17–20

Set aside dedicated time each week for revision. Access Eduooz Academy's recorded sessions to revisit challenging topics and clarify doubts. Use flashcards, quick revision notes, and spaced repetition to reinforce high-yield concepts before the exam.

01

Online Application Submission

Candidates must apply online through the official JIPMER portal during the notified application period, filling in personal, academic, and professional details accurately.

02

Application Fee Payment

Pay the application fee online according to category (UR / OBC / EWS / SC / ST / Ex-Servicemen). Payment is required to complete the application process successfully.

03

Application Status & Document Upload

After successful registration, candidates can check their application status and upload scanned photographs, signatures, and other required documents as per JIPMER guidelines.

04

Entrance Examination (CBT / Written Test)

Eligible candidates appear for the Computer-Based Test (CBT) or written examination on the scheduled date. The exam assesses nursing knowledge, clinical skills, medical-surgical nursing, community health, mental health, and specialty nursing areas.

05

Shortlisting / Merit List Publication

Based on examination performance, candidates are shortlisted for document verification or appointment. Scores and merit lists are published on the official JIPMER website.

06

Document Verification & Medical Examination

Shortlisted candidates must produce original documents for verification and may undergo a medical fitness examination if required by the post. Educational, registration, and category certificates are verified.

07

Final Merit List & Appointment

The final merit list is prepared based on examination scores, document verification, and medical fitness. Selected candidates are appointed to Nursing Officer posts according to merit, reservation norms, and available vacancies.

Everything you need to know.

From curriculum specifics to international placement assistance, find the answers to your most pressing questions about JIPMER Nursing Officer exam preparation.

JIPMER Nursing Officer Recruitment is a competitive hiring process conducted by Jawaharlal Institute of Postgraduate Medical Education and Research (JIPMER) to recruit qualified Nursing Officers for its hospitals and healthcare facilities. The recruitment process typically includes a Computer-Based Test (CBT), document verification, and merit-based selection.

Candidates possessing B.Sc Nursing, Post Basic B.Sc Nursing, or General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM) qualifications along with valid Nursing Council registration are generally eligible to apply, subject to the latest recruitment notification.

The educational qualification for JIPMER Nursing Officer vacancies typically includes B.Sc Nursing, Post Basic B.Sc Nursing, or GNM Nursing from a recognized institution with valid registration as a Nurse and Midwife.

The JIPMER Nursing Officer selection process generally includes a Computer-Based Test (CBT), merit-based evaluation, document verification, and medical fitness assessment.

The JIPMER Nursing Officer Exam Pattern usually consists of objective-type multiple-choice questions covering nursing subjects, general awareness, reasoning ability, quantitative aptitude, and English language proficiency.

Applicants generally need educational certificates, Nursing Council registration certificate, identity proof, age proof, category certificate (if applicable), passport-size photographs, signature, and other supporting documents.

JIPMER Nursing Officers provide patient care, administer medications, monitor patient conditions, assist doctors, maintain healthcare records, supervise nursing services, and ensure quality patient care.

JIPMER Nursing Officer jobs offer government job security, attractive salary packages, healthcare benefits, paid leave, pension-related benefits, research exposure, and excellent career growth opportunities.

JIPMER Nursing Officers can advance to Senior Nursing Officer, Assistant Nursing Superintendent, Deputy Nursing Superintendent, Nursing Superintendent, Nurse Educator, and other nursing leadership positions.

JIPMER Nursing Officer jobs offer stable central government employment, competitive salaries, professional development opportunities, advanced clinical exposure, and the opportunity to work in one of India's leading medical institutions.
