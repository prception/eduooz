What is Kuwait Nursing Prometric?

The Kuwait Nursing Prometric (KMLE) is the mandatory licensing examination for internationally trained nurses seeking to practice in Kuwait. Conducted by Prometric on behalf of the Ministry of Health, Kuwait, it is a computer-based test (CBT) that evaluates nursing competency for GCC healthcare practice.

01

Exam Booking via Prometric

Register on the Prometric official portal and schedule your Kuwait Nursing Prometric (KMLE) exam at an authorized Prometric testing center. Ensure your nursing qualification and council registration documents are ready. Pay the exam fee and receive your exam confirmation and scheduling details.

02

Data Check / Quadrabay Verification (PSV)

Complete mandatory Primary Source Verification (PSV) through DataFlow or Quadrabay. All academic certificates, nursing council registration, clinical experience documents, and professional credentials are verified directly with the issuing institutions. This is a required step before your Kuwait nursing license can be issued.

03

License Registration with Kuwait MOH

After successfully passing the Prometric exam and completing the DataFlow / Quadrabay document verification, submit your nursing license registration application to the Ministry of Health, Kuwait. Upon approval, you receive your official Kuwait nursing license, enabling you to practice as a Registered Nurse in Kuwait's healthcare system.

The Kuwait Nursing Prometric exam, officially known as the KMLE (Kuwait Medical Licensing Examination), is a mandatory computer-based licensing test conducted by Prometric on behalf of the Ministry of Health, Kuwait. It must be passed by internationally trained nurses before they can receive a Kuwait nursing license to practice.

Nurses with B.Sc Nursing, Post Basic B.Sc Nursing, or GNM qualification along with valid and active registration from their home country's nursing council are eligible. A valid passport and relevant clinical experience are also required. GNM candidates typically need minimum 2 years of experience.

The Kuwait nursing licensing process has 3 main steps: (1) Book and appear for the Kuwait Prometric (KMLE) exam at an authorized Prometric testing center. (2) Complete Primary Source Verification (PSV) through DataFlow or Quadrabay. (3) Submit your license registration application to the Ministry of Health, Kuwait, and receive your official nursing license upon approval.

DataFlow and Quadrabay are Primary Source Verification (PSV) agencies that verify the authenticity of your academic degrees, nursing council registration, and professional credentials directly with the issuing institutions. Kuwait MOH requires this verification as a mandatory step before issuing a nursing license to internationally trained nurses.

The Kuwait Prometric exam covers core nursing subjects including Medical-Surgical Nursing, OBG and Pediatrics, Community Health Nursing, Mental Health Nursing, Pharmacology, Fundamental of Nursing, Anatomy & Physiology, Infection Control, and Professional Nursing Ethics. The exam focuses on clinical judgment and applied nursing competency.

The Kuwait Nursing Prometric (KMLE) is a Computer-Based Test (CBT) conducted at authorized Prometric testing centers. The exam consists of multiple-choice questions (MCQs) that test clinical nursing knowledge and judgment. Candidates schedule their exam through the Prometric website and appear at their chosen test center.

Yes, GNM (General Nursing and Midwifery) diploma holders can appear for the Kuwait Nursing Prometric exam. GNM candidates are typically required to have a minimum of 2 years of clinical nursing experience in a recognized hospital, along with valid registration from their home country's nursing council, to qualify for the Kuwait licensing process.

Documents typically required include: valid passport, educational certificates (nursing degree/diploma), nursing council registration certificate, clinical experience letter, DataFlow/Quadrabay PSV report, Prometric exam result, passport-size photographs, and a medical fitness certificate. Always check the latest requirements from the Kuwait Ministry of Health.
