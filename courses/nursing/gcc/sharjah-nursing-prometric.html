What is GCC NURSING - Sharjah Nursing Prometric Licensing Exam?

GCC Nursing – Sharjah Nursing Prometric Licensing Exam is a leading international nursing licensure preparation program designed for nurses aiming to practice in Sharjah and other Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) countries. The Sharjah Prometric Nursing License is a highly recognized GCC nursing credential, required to work as a Registered Nurse (RN) or Licensed Nurse (LN) in hospitals, clinics, and healthcare facilities across Sharjah.

01

DataFlow Submission

Candidates begin the licensing process by completing Primary Source Verification (PSV) through DataFlow. Documents verified include educational qualifications, nursing registration certificates, professional experience documents, and supporting credentials.

02

Examination Booking and Attendance

Eligible candidates schedule and appear for the Sharjah Nursing Prometric Examination at an authorized testing center. The examination evaluates nursing knowledge, clinical competency, professional nursing practice, and patient care skills.

03

Eligibility Confirmation

After successfully completing the examination and verification process, candidates receive eligibility approval from the Sharjah healthcare authority.

04

License Registration

Following employment and regulatory approval, candidates complete the final licensing process and obtain authorization to practice nursing in Sharjah healthcare facilities.

The Sharjah Nursing Prometric Exam is a licensing examination required for nurses who want to work in healthcare facilities in Sharjah, UAE. It is conducted to assess nursing competency and is part of the professional licensing process under the UAE healthcare regulatory system.

The Sharjah Nursing Licensing Exam is a professional assessment test used to evaluate the clinical knowledge, skills, and competency of nurses before granting approval to practice in hospitals, clinics, and medical centers in Sharjah.

Candidates with GNM Nursing, B.Sc Nursing, Post Basic B.Sc Nursing, or equivalent nursing qualifications along with relevant clinical experience are generally eligible to apply for the Sharjah Nursing Prometric Exam.

Yes, most nursing categories require a minimum of 2 years of clinical experience after qualification. However, requirements may vary depending on job role and UAE licensing regulations.

Candidates can apply through the UAE licensing system (MOHAP or relevant authority portal), complete DataFlow verification, submit documents, and book the Prometric exam through authorized testing centers.

The Sharjah Nursing Prometric Exam is a computer-based multiple-choice question (MCQ) test covering nursing fundamentals, medical-surgical nursing, pharmacology, pediatric nursing, obstetrics, and patient care management.

Required documents include educational certificates, passport copy, nursing registration certificate, experience certificates, passport-size photograph, and other supporting documents for verification.

After passing the exam, candidates can work as Staff Nurses, ICU Nurses, Emergency Nurses, OT Nurses, and specialized nurses in Sharjah hospitals and healthcare centers.

The Sharjah Nursing Exam offers international career opportunities, tax-free salary, job security, advanced healthcare exposure, and professional growth in the UAE healthcare system.
