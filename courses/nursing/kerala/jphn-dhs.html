What is Kerala PSC JPHN DHS?

The Kerala PSC Junior Public Health Nurse (JPHN) Exam is conducted by the Kerala Public Service Commission (PSC) to recruit qualified nurses for the position of Junior Public Health Nurse in the public health sector. This role is vital for providing nursing services in rural and urban health centers, community health programs, and other public health initiatives under the Kerala State Government.

The Kerala PSC JPHN DHS Exam is a government recruitment examination conducted for the position of Junior Public Health Nurse (JPHN) under the Directorate of Health Services (DHS), Kerala. This exam is one of the most awaited opportunities for nursing professionals seeking a stable and respected career in the public health system of Kerala.

01

Online Application Submission

Candidates must apply online through the official Kerala PSC portal during the notified application period, filling in personal, academic, and professional details accurately as per the advertisement.

02

Application Fee Payment

Pay the application fee online according to the post or category (UR/OBC/EWS/SC/ST/Ex-Servicemen). Payment is required to complete the registration process as per Kerala PSC guidelines.

03

Application Status & Document Upload

After successful registration, candidates can check application status and upload scanned photographs, signature, and other required documents as per Kerala PSC guidelines and post requirements.

04

Entrance Examination (CBT / Written Test)

Eligible candidates appear for the Computer-Based Test (CBT) or written examination on the scheduled date. The exam assesses subject knowledge relevant to nursing including medical-surgical nursing, community health, maternal-child nursing, and general skills.

05

Shortlisting for Merit List / Score Publication

Based on exam performance, candidates are shortlisted for counselling or appointment. Scores and merit lists are published on the official Kerala PSC website for candidates to verify their selection status.

06

Document Verification & Medical Examination

Shortlisted candidates must produce original documents for verification and may undergo a medical fitness check if required. Educational certificates, nursing registration, and category documents are thoroughly verified.

07

Final Merit List & Appointment

The final merit list is prepared based on exam scores, document verification, and medical fitness. Selected candidates are appointed as Nursing Officers in Delhi government hospitals and healthcare units according to merit, reservation norms, and available vacancies.

Everything you need to know.

From eligibility to exam preparation strategies, find answers to your most pressing questions about the Kerala PSC JPHN DHS exam.

JPHN DHS stands for Junior Public Health Nurse under the Directorate of Health Services (DHS). A JPHN plays a vital role in community healthcare by providing maternal and child health services, immunization support, health education, disease prevention, and public health awareness programs.

Candidates applying for JPHN DHS recruitment generally must have Plus Two qualification, ANM (Auxiliary Nurse Midwife), General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM), or other qualifications specified in the official recruitment notification along with valid Nursing Council registration where applicable.

The educational qualification for JPHN DHS vacancies varies by state and recruitment rules but commonly includes ANM, Nursing qualifications, or equivalent healthcare certifications recognized by the concerned authorities.

The JPHN DHS selection process may include a written examination, merit-based selection, document verification, interview, or rank list preparation depending on the recruitment authority.

A JPHN under DHS is responsible for immunization programs, antenatal care, postnatal care, maternal health services, child healthcare, health surveys, disease prevention activities, and public health awareness campaigns.

Applicants generally need educational certificates, identity proof, age proof, Nursing Council registration certificate (if applicable), category certificate, domicile certificate, passport-size photographs, and other supporting documents.

JPHN DHS jobs offer government job security, attractive salary packages, healthcare benefits, paid leave, pension benefits (where applicable), and opportunities for career advancement in public health services.

Most JPHN DHS recruitment drives do not require prior experience, making them suitable for fresh candidates who meet the eligibility criteria.

A JPHN can progress to Senior Public Health Nurse, Health Supervisor, Public Health Inspector, Community Health Officer, Staff Nurse, and other higher healthcare positions through experience and promotions.

JPHN DHS government jobs offer stable employment, competitive salaries, public service opportunities, healthcare benefits, career growth, and the chance to contribute to community health and welfare programs.
