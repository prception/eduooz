What is Kerala PSC Assistant Professor in Nursing?

The Kerala PSC Assistant Professor in Nursing Exam is a state-level recruitment conducted by the Kerala Public Service Commission to select qualified nursing educators for government nursing colleges and universities across Kerala. The process evaluates subject expertise, teaching aptitude, and academic experience required for faculty appointments.

01

Online Application Submission

Candidates must apply online through the official Kerala PSC portal during the notified application period, filling in personal, academic, and professional details accurately as per the advertisement.

02

Application Fee Payment

Pay the application fee online according to the post or category (UR/OBC/EWS/SC/ST/Ex-Servicemen). Payment is required to complete the registration process as per Kerala PSC guidelines.

03

Application Status & Document Upload

After successful registration, candidates can check application status and upload scanned photographs, signature, and other required documents as per Kerala PSC guidelines and post requirements.

04

Entrance Examination (CBT / Written Test)

Eligible candidates appear for the Computer-Based Test (CBT) or written examination on the scheduled date. The exam assesses subject knowledge relevant to nursing including medical-surgical nursing, community health, maternal-child nursing, and general skills.

05

Shortlisting for Merit List / Score Publication

Based on exam performance, candidates are shortlisted for interview/selection stages. Scores and merit lists are published on the official Kerala PSC website for candidates to verify their selection status.

06

Document Verification & Medical Examination

Shortlisted candidates must produce original documents for verification and may undergo a medical fitness check if required. Educational certificates, nursing registration, and category documents are thoroughly verified.

07

Final Merit List & Appointment

The final merit list is prepared based on exam scores, document verification, and medical fitness. Selected candidates are appointed as Assistant Professors in Kerala government nursing colleges and institutions according to merit, reservation norms, and available vacancies.

Everything you need to know.

From APN eligibility to exam preparation strategies, find answers to your most pressing questions about the Kerala PSC Assistant Professor in Nursing exam.

An Assistant Professor in Nursing is a nursing educator responsible for teaching undergraduate and postgraduate nursing students, conducting research, supervising clinical training, and contributing to academic development in nursing colleges and healthcare institutions.

Candidates applying for Assistant Professor in Nursing jobs generally must possess an M.Sc Nursing degree from a recognized institution. Some organizations may also require teaching experience, research publications, or additional qualifications as per recruitment rules.

The minimum qualification for Assistant Professor in Nursing vacancies is typically M.Sc Nursing in the relevant specialty. Certain universities may prefer candidates with Ph.D. Nursing qualifications.

Candidates can apply online through the official Kerala PSC One Time Registration (OTR) portal by completing the application form, uploading required documents, and submitting within the prescribed application period.

The selection process may include a written examination, academic score evaluation, interview, teaching demonstration, research assessment, and document verification as per the Kerala PSC notification.

An Assistant Professor in Nursing is responsible for teaching nursing subjects, preparing lesson plans, conducting research, mentoring students, supervising clinical practice, evaluating academic performance, and participating in curriculum development.

Yes, valid registration with the State Nursing Council or Indian Nursing Council is generally required for Assistant Professor in Nursing recruitment as per the official notification.

Candidates typically need educational certificates, M.Sc Nursing degree certificate, Nursing Council registration certificate, experience certificates, research publication details, identity proof, and category certificates if applicable.

Career advancement opportunities include Associate Professor in Nursing, Professor in Nursing, Head of Department (HOD), Dean of Nursing, Principal of Nursing College, and Nursing Researcher.

Assistant Professor in Nursing jobs offer stable academic careers, excellent salary packages, opportunities for teaching and research, professional development, leadership roles, and long-term career growth in nursing education.
