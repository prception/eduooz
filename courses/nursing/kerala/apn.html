Everything you need to know.

From eligibility and KNMC registration to exam pattern and career growth — find answers to your most important Kerala PSC APN questions.

The Kerala PSC APN Exam is a competitive written examination conducted by the Kerala Public Service Commission (Kerala PSC) for the recruitment of Auxiliary Public Health Nurses in the Department of Health Services (DHS), Government of Kerala. Selected candidates are posted in Primary Health Centres (PHCs), Community Health Centres (CHCs), and Health Sub-Centres across all districts of Kerala.

Candidates who have passed GNM (General Nursing & Midwifery), B.Sc Nursing, or Post Basic B.Sc Nursing from a recognized institution, along with valid registration with the Kerala Nurses and Midwives Council (KNMC), are eligible to apply for the APN post. Candidates must also meet the age limit specified in the Kerala PSC notification.

The minimum educational qualification for the Kerala PSC APN post is GNM (General Nursing and Midwifery) from a recognized institution affiliated to the Kerala Nurses and Midwives Council (KNMC) or the Indian Nursing Council (INC). Candidates with B.Sc Nursing and Post Basic B.Sc Nursing are also eligible. A valid RNRM registration with KNMC is mandatory.

The general upper age limit for Kerala PSC APN recruitment is 41 years. Age relaxation is provided as per Kerala PSC rules: SC/ST candidates get 5 additional years (upper limit 46 years), OBC/SEBC candidates get 3 additional years (upper limit 44 years), Persons with Disabilities (PH/PwD) get up to 10 additional years, and Ex-Servicemen are entitled to relaxation as per Kerala Government rules.

Yes, valid and active registration with the Kerala Nurses and Midwives Council (KNMC) as a Registered Nurse and Registered Midwife (RNRM) is mandatory for APN recruitment under DHS Kerala. Candidates must maintain their KNMC registration throughout the recruitment process and during service. Candidates who have completed training but are awaiting registration may be permitted to apply, subject to the specific notification conditions.

The Kerala PSC APN examination is a written OMR-based objective type test. It typically consists of 100 multiple-choice questions (MCQs) covering nursing and health science subjects, to be answered in 75 minutes (1 hour 15 minutes). Each correct answer carries 1 mark. There is negative marking of 1/3 mark for each wrong answer, as per Kerala PSC standard examination rules. Candidates must refer to the latest notification for exact details.

The Kerala PSC APN syllabus covers: Community Health Nursing, Maternal and Child Health (MCH), Fundamentals of Nursing, Medical-Surgical Nursing, Child Health Nursing (Paediatrics), Mental Health and Psychiatric Nursing, Nutrition and Dietetics, Environmental Health and Sanitation, Primary Health Care concepts, National Health Programmes, and General Knowledge about Kerala's Health Services. The syllabus follows the INC GNM curriculum standards.

The APN post in DHS Kerala falls under the Kerala Government Pay Revision scale, with a basic pay starting from approximately ₹18,000 per month (Pay Level 10). In addition to the basic pay, APNs receive Dearness Allowance (DA), House Rent Allowance (HRA), medical benefits, paid leave, pension, and other service benefits as per Kerala Government rules. The total monthly emoluments including allowances can be significantly higher than the basic pay.

Auxiliary Public Health Nurses in DHS Kerala have a structured career progression path. They can advance to Junior Public Health Nurse (JPHN), Senior Health Inspector, Public Health Nurse, and higher-level administrative nursing positions through departmental promotions and seniority-based advancement. Opportunities exist to specialize in MCH programs, immunization drives, National Health Mission (NHM) activities, and community health campaigns. Higher studies such as B.Sc Nursing and M.Sc Nursing can further accelerate career growth.
