What is Kerala PSC Staff Nurse DHS?

The Kerala PSC Staff Nurse DHS Exam is a state-level recruitment examination conducted by the Kerala Public Service Commission to select qualified nurses for the Department of Health Services, Kerala. It is the essential pathway for nurses aiming to join Kerala DHS hospitals, community health centers, and state healthcare institutions.

Everything you need to know.

From Kerala PSC eligibility to exam preparation strategies, find answers to your most pressing questions about the Staff Nurse DHS exam.

The Kerala PSC Staff Nurse DHS Exam is a competitive recruitment examination conducted by the Kerala Public Service Commission (Kerala PSC) for the recruitment of staff nurse positions in various Kerala DHS hospitals, health departments, and healthcare institutions under the Government of Kerala.

Candidates possessing B.Sc Nursing, Post Basic B.Sc Nursing, GNM Nursing, or equivalent nursing qualifications along with valid Nursing Council registration are generally eligible to apply for Kerala PSC Staff Nurse DHS recruitment.

The educational qualification for Kerala PSC Staff Nurse DHS vacancies generally includes B.Sc Nursing, Post Basic B.Sc Nursing, or Diploma in General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM) from a recognized institution with valid registration.

The Kerala PSC Staff Nurse DHS selection process generally includes a Computer-Based Test (CBT), merit-based shortlisting, document verification, and medical fitness verification.

The Kerala PSC Staff Nurse DHS Exam Pattern typically consists of objective-type multiple-choice questions covering nursing subjects, general awareness, reasoning ability, numerical aptitude, and language proficiency.

Applicants generally need educational certificates, Nursing Council registration certificate, identity proof, age proof, category certificate (if applicable), passport-size photographs, signature, and other supporting documents.

Kerala PSC Staff Nurse DHS nurses provide patient care, administer medications, assist doctors, monitor patient conditions, maintain medical records, supervise nursing activities, and ensure quality healthcare services.

Kerala PSC Staff Nurse DHS jobs offer government job security, attractive salary packages, healthcare benefits, paid leave, pension-related benefits, promotion opportunities, and long-term career stability.

Nursing Officers can advance to Senior Nursing Officer, Nursing Superintendent, Assistant Nursing Superintendent, Deputy Nursing Superintendent, and various nursing administration roles.

Kerala PSC Staff Nurse DHS jobs offer stable government employment, competitive salaries, excellent benefits, career advancement opportunities, and the chance to work in reputed Kerala DHS hospitals and healthcare institutions.
