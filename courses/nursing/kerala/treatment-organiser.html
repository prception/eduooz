About the Kerala PSC TREATMENT ORGANISER EXAM

The Kerala PSC Treatment Organiser Exam Coaching is a specialized training program designed to help candidates prepare effectively for the Kerala Public Service Commission (PSC) Treatment Organiser recruitment examination.

This coaching equips aspirants with the knowledge, skills, and exam strategies needed to succeed in this competitive government job selection process.

A Kerala PSC Treatment Organiser is a healthcare support role under the Kerala Public Service Commission that involves assisting in clinical settings, coordinating basic patient care activities, and supporting medical professionals in treatment delivery across government health institutions.

Who Can Apply

Eligibility Criteria

Educational Qualification

Educational Qualification

Candidates must possess any one of the following qualifications from a recognized institution:

  • Diploma in General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM)
  • Bachelor of Science in Nursing (B.Sc Nursing)
  • Relevant Paramedical or Health Sciences Diploma as prescribed in the official notification

Registration

Candidates with nursing qualifications must possess valid registration with the Kerala Nurses and Midwives Council (KNMC). Candidates with paramedical qualifications may require registration with the relevant professional council as specified in the notification.

Age Limit

Candidates should generally be between 18 and 36 years of age. Age relaxation is available for eligible reserved categories as per Kerala PSC rules.

Age Relaxation

Scheduled Caste (SC) / Scheduled Tribe (ST)

5 Years

Age relaxation of 5 years for SC/ST candidates as per Kerala PSC norms.

Other Backward Classes (OBC - Non-Creamy Layer)

5 Years

OBC candidates receive 5 years of age relaxation under Kerala PSC rules.

Persons with Disabilities (PwD)

10 Years

PwD candidates receive up to 10 years of age relaxation as prescribed by Kerala PSC.

Below Poverty Line (BPL)

3 Years

BPL candidates may be eligible for 3 years of relaxation depending on notification rules.

Other PwBD Candidates

5 Years

Other PwBD categories may receive 5 years of relaxation as per notification provisions.

Ex-Servicemen

Up to 5 Years

Ex-Servicemen receive age relaxation equal to defence service and unemployment period after discharge, up to a maximum of 5 years.

01

Application Submission

Candidates must register through the Kerala PSC One Time Registration (OTR) portal and submit their applications online within the prescribed period.

02

Screening of Applications

All submitted applications are scrutinized to verify educational qualifications, age, registration status, and other eligibility conditions mentioned in the notification.

03

Written / OMR / Online Examination

Eligible candidates may be required to appear for a Written Test, OMR Examination, or Online Test that assesses healthcare knowledge, public health awareness, and job-related competencies.

04

Confirmation for Examination

Candidates must confirm their intention to attend the examination through their PSC profile. Only confirmed candidates can download admission tickets.

05

Certificate Verification

Based on examination performance, shortlisted candidates may be called for certificate verification to validate educational qualifications, registrations, age proof, and other supporting documents.

06

Final Selection

Final selection is based on examination performance, certificate verification, fulfillment of eligibility conditions, and inclusion in the Kerala PSC rank list.

FAQs

Treatment Organiser Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is a Treatment Organiser?

A Treatment Organiser is a healthcare professional responsible for coordinating patient treatment plans, monitoring treatment adherence, maintaining medical records, supporting disease control programs, and assisting healthcare teams in government hospitals, public health departments, and healthcare institutions.

2. What are the eligibility criteria for Treatment Organiser recruitment?

Candidates applying for Treatment Organiser recruitment must meet the educational qualifications, age requirements, and other eligibility conditions specified in the official recruitment notification issued by the concerned department.

3. What is the selection process for Treatment Organiser vacancies?

The Treatment Organiser selection process may include a written examination, merit-based selection, interview, document verification, or skill assessment depending on the recruitment authority.

4. What educational qualification is required for Treatment Organiser jobs?

The educational qualification for Treatment Organiser vacancies generally includes Plus Two, Diploma, Degree, Nursing qualification, Health Inspector qualification, or other healthcare-related qualifications as prescribed in the recruitment notification.

5. What are the duties and responsibilities of a Treatment Organiser?

A Treatment Organiser is responsible for tracking patient treatment progress, maintaining treatment records, coordinating healthcare services, supporting disease control programs, ensuring treatment compliance, and assisting medical officers in healthcare management.

6. What documents are required for Treatment Organiser application?

Applicants generally need educational certificates, identity proof, age proof, experience certificate (if applicable), category certificate, passport-size photographs, and other documents mentioned in the recruitment notification.

7. What are the benefits of working as a Treatment Organiser?

Treatment Organiser jobs provide healthcare sector experience, government employment opportunities, professional development, community service experience, and career advancement prospects.

8. What are the career growth opportunities for a Treatment Organiser?

Treatment Organisers can advance to positions in public health programs, healthcare administration, disease control projects, health supervision roles, and other healthcare management positions based on experience and qualifications.

9. What is the role of a Treatment Organiser in healthcare programs?

A Treatment Organiser helps ensure successful implementation of healthcare programs by monitoring patients, coordinating treatment schedules, supporting follow-up care, and maintaining accurate healthcare records.

10. Why should I apply for Treatment Organiser jobs?

Treatment Organiser jobs offer an opportunity to work in the healthcare sector, contribute to public health programs, gain valuable experience, receive competitive salaries, and build a rewarding career in healthcare administration.
