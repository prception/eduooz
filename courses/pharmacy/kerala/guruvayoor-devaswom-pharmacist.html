What is Guruvayoor Devaswom Pharmacist?

The Pharmacist Grade II position under the Guruvayoor Devaswom Board is a government-regulated role recruited through the Kerala Devaswom Recruitment Board (KDRB). A Pharmacist at the Guruvayoor Devaswom Medical Center plays a critical role in ensuring the smooth operation of the institution's healthcare services, which cater to both the temple staff and, in many cases, a significant volume of pilgrims.

All submitted applications are screened to verify eligibility based on educational qualifications, age, reservation, and other criteria specified in the notification.

03

A written, OMR, or online test may be conducted to assess candidates' general knowledge, analytical ability, and subject-related knowledge relevant to the Pharmacist post.

Confirmation for Exam

Candidates must confirm their intention to appear for the exam through their One Time Registration profile. Only confirmed candidates can download their admission tickets.

05

Certificate Verification

Based on the written test result, eligible candidates may be called for certificate verification to validate educational documents, community status, reservation qualifications, and other claims.

Final Selection

Final selection is based on written exam performance and compliance with all eligibility criteria, reservation rules, and other conditions prescribed under Guruvayoor Devaswom Board rules.

Everything you need to know.

From eligibility criteria to career growth, find the answers to your most pressing questions about the Guruvayoor Devaswom Board Pharmacist exam.

The Guruvayoor Devaswom Board Exam is a competitive examination conducted for selecting eligible candidates for various posts under the Guruvayoor Devaswom Board, which manages the famous Guruvayoor Temple and its associated institutions.

Eligibility varies by post. Generally, candidates with SSLC, Plus Two, Degree, Diploma, or professional qualifications such as B.Ed, Nursing, or technical certifications are eligible depending on the specific post.

The minimum qualification depends on the post and may include SSLC, Higher Secondary (Plus Two), Graduation, Post Graduation, or professional degrees from recognized institutions.

The selection process generally includes a written examination, interview, skill test (if applicable), and document verification.

The Pharmacist post under the Guruvayoor Devaswom Board carries a pay scale of ₹32,600 to ₹74,800 per month, along with allowances and benefits applicable to Devaswom Board employees.

The exam pattern usually includes objective-type multiple-choice questions covering general knowledge, reasoning ability, Malayalam language, arithmetic ability, and subject-specific topics depending on the post.

Applicants generally need educational certificates, age proof, identity proof, category certificate (if applicable), passport-size photograph, and signature.

Employees can be promoted to higher administrative positions, supervisory roles, and departmental leadership posts based on experience and performance.

Skills include general knowledge, communication ability, Malayalam language proficiency, administrative knowledge, and subject-specific expertise.

It offers stable government employment, cultural and administrative service opportunities, job security, and long-term career growth in a reputed temple management institution.
