What is the Bahrain Pharmacy Prometric Exam?

GCC Pharmacy – Bahrain Prometric Licensing Exam is a specialized international licensure preparation program designed for pharmacists seeking to practice in Bahrain. NHRA (National Health Regulatory Authority) is a widely recognized GCC credential, required to work as a Licensed Pharmacist / Pharmacy Technician / Clinical Pharmacist in hospitals, clinics, and healthcare facilities across Bahrain. This program ensures candidates meet the professional standards and regulatory requirements set by the NHRA and promotes excellence in pharmacy practice across the region.

Everything you need to know.

From eligibility criteria to exam format and licensing validity, find the answers to your most pressing questions about the Bahrain Pharmacy Prometric exam.

The Bahrain Pharmacy Prometric Exam is a licensing examination conducted for pharmacists who wish to practice in the Kingdom of Bahrain. It evaluates candidates' pharmaceutical knowledge, clinical skills, and competency to ensure safe and effective pharmacy practice in hospitals, clinics, and community pharmacies.

Candidates with a Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm), Pharm.D, or equivalent degree from a recognized institution, along with valid registration from their home country's Pharmacy Council, are eligible to apply.

Yes, most categories require minimum post-qualification experience, usually 1–2 years in hospital or community pharmacy settings, depending on NHRA regulations.

DataFlow is a primary source verification process required to authenticate educational certificates, pharmacy registration, and professional experience before eligibility for the exam is approved.

The exam is a computer-based test (CBT) consisting of multiple-choice questions (MCQs) covering pharmaceutical sciences, clinical pharmacy, pharmacology, pharmaceutics and pharmaceutical chemistry, and drug information and patient counseling.

The Bahrain pharmacy license is valid for 1–2 years and must be renewed according to NHRA regulations.

The exam is moderately difficult and focuses on both theoretical knowledge and practical pharmacy application relevant to Bahrain healthcare standards.

Bahrain licensing opens opportunities to work in hospitals, community pharmacies, and healthcare institutions with high tax-free salaries, career growth, and international exposure.

Preparation tips include studying standard pharmacy textbooks, reviewing clinical and hospital pharmacy practices, practicing MCQs and mock tests, and understanding Bahrain pharmacy regulations and NHRA guidelines.
