What is DHA Pharmacy Prometric?

DHA Pharmacy Prometric Licensing Exam is a premier international pharmacy licensure preparation program for pharmacists who aim to practice in the United Arab Emirates, particularly in Dubai. The DHA Pharmacy Prometric License is a highly recognized GCC pharmacy credential required to work as a licensed pharmacist in hospitals, clinics, retail pharmacies, and healthcare facilities across Dubai.

Everything you need to know.

From DataFlow verification to licensing validity, find the answers to your most pressing questions about the DHA Pharmacy Prometric Licensing Exam.

The DHA Pharmacy Prometric Exam is a licensing examination conducted by the Dubai Health Authority (DHA) for pharmacists who wish to practice in Dubai. It evaluates candidates' pharmaceutical knowledge, clinical skills, and competency to ensure safe and effective pharmacy practice in UAE healthcare facilities.

Candidates with a Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm), Pharm.D, or equivalent pharmacy degree from a recognized institution, along with registration from their home country's Pharmacy Council, are eligible to apply.

Yes, most pharmacy categories require at least 2 years of post-qualification clinical experience. Experience requirements vary depending on the DHA pharmacy license category.

The DHA Pharmacy Exam is a computer-based test (CBT) consisting of multiple-choice questions (MCQs) covering pharmacy practice, pharmacology, pharmaceutics, pharmaceutical chemistry, clinical pharmacy, and patient safety.

The exam is moderately difficult and evaluates both theoretical knowledge and practical understanding of pharmacy practice in line with UAE healthcare standards.

Applicants need:

  • Educational certificates (B.Pharm/Pharm.D)
  • Home country pharmacy registration
  • Passport copy
  • Experience certificate
  • Passport-size photograph
  • Valid ID proof

DHA licensing opens opportunities to work in Dubai hospitals, community pharmacies, and healthcare institutions with attractive tax-free salaries, career growth, and international exposure.

Preparation tips include:

  • Studying standard pharmacy textbooks
  • Reviewing clinical pharmacy and hospital pharmacy practices
  • Practicing MCQs and mock tests
  • Understanding UAE pharmacy regulations and laws

The DHA pharmacy license is valid for 1 2 years and must be renewed according to Dubai Health Authority regulations.

Most DHA pharmacy licenses require post-qualification experience, so fresh graduates are generally not eligible.
